I work at the intersection of technology, culture, and imagination.

My research asks how artificial intelligence reshapes not only what we create, but what we recognize as creativity itself—who is seen as an author, what counts as agency, and how value is assigned within cultural systems.

I am a faculty member in the Department of Technology, AI, and Society at Stony Brook University. I received my Ph.D. from The Ohio State University, and have taught at Ohio State and Columbia University.

Before entering academia, I worked in cultural institutions and the entertainment industry—spaces where creativity, infrastructure, and power are constantly negotiated.
